# Lecture
#Lecture 
## Why Privacy Matters
#Privacy
- Data is a powerful asset - Companies and hackers want it
- You generate data every day without realising
- From online searches to fitness apps - Your digital footprint matters

## Digital Identity and Data Trails
#Digital-Identity #Data-Trails
- Your online behaviour forms a digital profile
- Businesses use this to target products, offers, services
- Example: Supermarket loyalty cards and health profiles

## Where is your Data?
#Data 
- Cloud storage = Your data stored on someone else's computer
- Files, images, chats, location - Stored and monetised
- Most users don't read terms before uploading

## Privacy in the Workplace
- Employer may monitor email, browsing, location
- Onsite Wi-Fi and work devices are not private
- Important to maintain professionalism at all times

## Example: Centrelink and Robodebt
#Example 
- Automated income assessment caused widespread harm
- Data matching gone wrong - Lack of oversight
- Led to public backlash and class action lawsuits

## Example: Cambridge Analytica
#Example 
- Harvested Facebook data from millions without consent
- Used for political profiling and influence
- Sparked global conversation on data ethics

## Privacy Principles
#Privacy-Principles
- Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) govern data use
- Cover collection, consent, storage, access, correction
- Not following these can result in legal action

### APP 1 - 13 Overview
#APP 
1. Open and transparent management of personal info
2. Anonymity and pseudonymity options
3. Collection must be lawful and necessary
4. Personal info must be kept secure
5. Clear notification when data is collected
6. Use and disclosure must match purpose of collection
7. Direct marketing limits
8. Overseas disclosure rules
9. Accuracy
10. Access
11. Correction
12. Complaints
13. Handling

## Data Breaches and Responsibilities
#Data-Breach #Responsibilities
- Organisations must notify affected people if data is breached
- The Notifiable Data Breach (NDB) scheme requires action
- Failing to act can harm reputation and lead to penalties

## What is ethical use of data?
#Ethics 
- Just because you can collect it, doesn't mean you should
- Respecting people's autonomy and rights is key
- Need to balance innovation and privacy

## Ethics vs Law
#Ethics #Law
- Legal ≠ Ethical: Something can be lawful but still wrong
- Eg: Microtargeting political ads based on mental health data
- Professionals must reflect on impact of their work

## AI and Privacy
#AI #Privacy 
- AI systems ingest and analyse massive amounts of personal data
- Bias and discrimination can arise from training data
- Transparency and explainability are critical

## Example: AI in Hiring
#Example 
- AI Tools may reject candidates based on irrelevant traits
- If data is biased, outcomes are biased
- Companies must audit and validate systems

## Professional Responsibility
#Responsibilities 
- As future IT Professionals, you'll be entrusted with sensitive systems
- Responsibility to protect data and design for fairness
- Build systems that don't cause harm

## Code of Conduct
#Code-of-Conduct
- Many employers, universities and industries enforce a code
- Sets standards for professional behaviour
- Breach can result in disciplinary action or job loss

## Examples of Misconduct
#Example 
- Accessing private customer data out of curiosity
- Failing to secure passwords or logins
- Posting internal client systems on social media

## Example: The curious employee
#Example 
- Staffer accesses celebrity's health records without cause
- Violation of policy and trust
- Result: Termination and possible legal consequences

## Your Digital Reputation
#Reputation
- What you post online can affect your career
- Employers review social media during hiring
- Private isn't always private - Screenshots, Leaks happen

## Academic Integrity
#Integrity
- Same ethical principles apply to study
- Plagiarism, cheating, ghostwriting violate trust
- Consequences: Warnings, fail grades, expulsion

## Ethical Decision Making Model
#EDMM
1. Recognise an ethical issue
2. Get the facts
3. Evaluate alternatives
4. Make and test a decision
5. Act and reflect

## Digital Consent and Transparency
#Consent #Transparency
- Clear terms and consent should be given when data is collected
- Avoid deceptive "Dark Patterns" in design
- Transparency builds user trust

## What is Privacy by Design
#Privacy 
- Incorporating privacy at the system design stage
- Not an afterthought - It's a foundation
- Minimise data, anonymise where possible
