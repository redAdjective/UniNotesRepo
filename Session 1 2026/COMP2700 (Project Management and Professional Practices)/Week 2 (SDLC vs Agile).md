# Lecture
#Lecture 
## Recap - What is SDLC?
- SDLC: Software/System Development Life Cycle
- Common phases: Planning, Analysis, Design, Implementation, Maintenance
- Structured method for delivering IT systems

## SDLC: One size does not fit all
#SDLC 
- Different Methodologies suit different projects
- Waterfall, Iterative, V-Model, Agile
- Context matters - Industry, scope, timeline

## Waterfall Methodology
#Waterfall
- Linear, Sequential approach
- Phases: Planning → Analysis → Design → Implementation → Maintenance
- Good for fixed, known requirements

### Cost of Late mistakes
#Risk 
- Early fix cost: 1x | Late fix cost: upto 40x
	Example: $100M project cancelled at 90% completion due to missing requirement

## Iterative Models
#Iterative
- Breakdown projects into smaller chunks
- Frequent refinement and continuous improvement
- Enables early detection of issues

## V-Model
#V-Model
- Extension of waterfall with built-in testing
- Each development stage maps to a testing phase
- Used where reliability is critical

## Rise of Agile
#Agile 
- Response to frustration with rigid SDLC
- Based on Agile Manifesto (2001)
- Encourages flexibility, customer focus, iterative delivery

## Agile vs Traditional Project Management
#Agile #Waterfall 
- Traditional: Project Charter, Fixed Scope and Timelines
- Agile: Sprints, Product Increments, Adaptive planning
- Focus on continuous feedback and working solutions

## Scrum: Key Agile Framework
#AgileScrum 
- Popular implementation of Agile
- Defined roles: Scrum Master, Product Owner, Development Team
- Iterative development via time-boxed Sprints

## Scrum Artefacts
#AgileScrum 
- Product Backlog - Master To-Do List
- Sprint Backlog - Work selected for sprint
- Burndown Chart - Visual of work remaining

## User Stories and Story Points
#User
- User story: "As a User, I want...."
- Story Points: Effort / Priority Indicator
- Helps prioritise and plan sprints

## Sprint Planning
#Sprint
- Team selects backlog items for sprint
- Defines what can be delivered in 2 - 4 weeks
- Assigns tasks based on skillsets and capacity

## Daily Stand-Ups
#Stand-Up
- Short daily meeting
- Share what was done, what's next and blockers
- Standing keeps it focused and fast

## Sprint Review and Retrospective
#Sprint 
- Review: Demonstrate deliverables to client
- Retrospective: What went well, what didn't, what to improve
- Example: Hot air balloon retrospective exercise

## Jira and Confluence
#Project-Management #Jira #Confluence
- Jira: Task tracking and Agile project management
- Confluence: Documentation and collaboration tool
- Used by major firms - adds to employability

## Kanban: A Visual Approach
#Project-Management #Kanban
- Boards: To-Do → In Progress → Done
- Great for personal productivity and team visibility
- Used in Jira and physical formats

## Wagile: The Hybrid approach
#Wagile
- Waterfall + Agile = Wagile
- Used in large organisations to satisfy both developers and finance
- Waterfall structure, Agile execution

### Why Wagile?
- Finance prefers fixed budget timelines
- Development teams want flexibility
- Wagile balances stakeholder needs

## When to use Waterfall
#Waterfall 
- Fixed, Well-defined requirements
- Military systems, bridges, hardware installations
- Minimal change tolerance

## When to use Agile
#Agile 
- Requirements may evolve
- Need ongoing client feedback
- Short, iterative delivery cycles

## Agile Core values
#Core-Values
1. Individuals and Interactions over processes/tools
2. Working software over documentation
3. Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
4. Responding to change over following a rigid plan

## Comparing Scope, Time and Cost
#Scope #Time #Cost 
- Waterfall: Fixed scope, time and cost
- Agile: Flexible scope, adaptive time and funding
- Scope creep managed through product backlog

## Example: 14 year project
#Example
- Finance project ran over 14 years (evolutionary)
- Long term projects can be agile or waterfall depending on needs
- Agile helps avoid stagnation
